Battles & Combat

Civil War Field Manual: Understanding Combat & Engagement

Introduction

Combat during the war was intense, disorganized, and overwhelming. Soldiers faced enemy fire, confusion, exhaustion, and the psychological strain of constant danger.

The following guide explains what battle was like, how armies fought, and the experiences soldiers endured on the field.

Overview: What Battle Was Like

Combat was chaotic, loud, and terrifying. Soldiers advanced in formation across open ground while under fire, often with limited visibility caused by thick clouds of gunpowder smoke.

  • Long marches before engagement
  • Smoke and confusion on the battlefield
  • Rigid line formations
  • Heavy casualties in short periods

Smoke and noise made coordination difficult.

Battlefield Tactics

Traditional Methods

Armies relied heavily on discipline and coordination. Soldiers fought in organized lines to maximize firepower despite the danger this created.

  • Line and column formations
  • Coordinated volleys of gunfire
  • Bayonet charges
  • Bugles and drums for commands

Evolving Warfare

As weapons improved, battlefield tactics slowly adapted to harsher realities.

  • Use of trenches and cover
  • Skirmish lines ahead of armies
  • Defensive positions
  • Entrenchments in long battles

The Soldier’s Experience

For individual soldiers, battle was often disorienting and horrifying. Explosions, smoke, gunfire, and wounded comrades created confusion and fear.

Units frequently lost cohesion, forcing soldiers to rely on instinct.

The cost of battle was severe and immediate.

Casualties & Impact

  • Heavy losses in major battles
  • Overwhelmed field hospitals
  • Disease following wounds
  • Long-term community impact

The Reality of Combat

While leadership and strategy shaped battles, the reality of combat remained brutal and unpredictable.

Soldiers endured physical hardship, emotional trauma, and constant uncertainty.
