Welcome to the Army

You’ve traded farm, workshop, or city street for a musket and a uniform. Whether you wear Union blue or Confederate gray, your life is about to become a mixture of boredom, fear, hardship, loyalty, and survival.

Most of your days will not be spent in glorious charges, but in drilling, marching, waiting, cleaning equipment, and trying not to get sick.

Training & Daily Routine

Learning to Be a Soldier

Training camps transform volunteers into organized military units. You will learn marching formations, weapon handling, firing drills, and how to move as part of a coordinated line.

  • Morning: Reveille, breakfast, roll call, first drill.
  • Day: Battalion drill, weapon maintenance, camp labor.
  • Evening: Dress parade, mail call, taps.

Discipline

  • Obey orders immediately
  • Maintain clean equipment
  • Be punctual for formation
Drill, Drill, Drill

Combat is rare compared to the endless rhythm of marching, formation practice, and camp maintenance.

Health & Hygiene

Important: Disease kills more Civil War soldiers than battlefield wounds.

Common Dangers

  • Dysentery and typhoid
  • Poor sanitation
  • Malnutrition
  • Contaminated water

Staying Healthy

  • Keep feet dry
  • Wash whenever possible
  • Use latrines properly
  • Boil water when available
Field Hospitals

Surgeons work under brutal conditions with limited medical knowledge and overcrowded facilities.
