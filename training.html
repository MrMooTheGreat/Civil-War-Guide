Military Training & Daily Life

Instructions for volunteer soldiers serving in camp during the War Between the States.

What Every Recruit Should Know

I. Follow Orders Quickly

Discipline kept armies organized during battle and camp life.

II. Maintain Equipment

Rifles, uniforms, and gear required constant cleaning and inspection.

III. Endure Routine

Soldiers repeated drills daily to prepare for combat conditions.

IV. Work Together

Armies depended upon coordination and cooperation between soldiers.

Training & Discipline

Training camps transformed civilians into organized military units. Recruits followed strict schedules and repeated military drills daily.

  • Morning reveille at dawn
  • Weapons drill and marching
  • Camp labor and inspections
  • Evening roll call

Soldiers practicing military drill.

A Soldier's Daily Schedule

Morning

Soldiers woke before sunrise for inspection and preparation.

Midday

Most daylight hours were spent drilling and marching.

Afternoon

Camp maintenance and equipment repair were completed.

Evening

The day ended with inspections, supper, and rest.

Military camp organization and order.

Discipline & Conduct

Soldiers were expected to obey commands immediately and maintain proper conduct at all times.

  • Strict obedience to orders
  • Regular inspections
  • Punishments for misconduct
  • Recognition for strong performance

Final Reflection

Training camps transformed ordinary civilians into disciplined soldiers capable of enduring military life.

Through repetition, structure, and cooperation, recruits developed the skills necessary for survival during war.
