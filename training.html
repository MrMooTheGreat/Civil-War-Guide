Guide For New Recruits

Instructions For Soldiers Entering Camp Service

Introduction

Camp life was strict, repetitive, and demanding. New recruits were expected to learn military discipline quickly in order to function effectively within the army.

The following guide explains the routines, duties, and expectations of soldiers during the Civil War.

General Rules For Recruits

  1. Obey all commands immediately and without argument.
  2. Keep weapons, uniforms, and equipment clean at all times.
  3. Arrive promptly for drill, inspection, and roll call.
  4. Maintain proper conduct within camp grounds.
  5. Support fellow soldiers and remain with your assigned unit.

Daily Routine In Camp

Time Of Day Duties
Morning Reveille, inspection, breakfast, and drill.
Midday Marching practice and weapons training.
Afternoon Camp labor, repairs, and cleaning duties.
Evening Roll call, supper, and final inspection.

Military Training

Soldiers practicing formation drill in camp.

Recruits spent many hours marching and drilling in formation. Repetition prepared soldiers to respond quickly during combat situations.

  • Weapons handling
  • Formation marching
  • Bayonet practice
  • Inspection procedures

Discipline & Conduct

Military discipline maintained order within camp. Officers expected soldiers to follow instructions immediately and maintain proper behavior.

Punishments were used to enforce discipline, while reliable soldiers often received greater responsibilities or promotion.

Final Notes

Army life required endurance, obedience, and cooperation. Soldiers who adapted quickly to military routine were more likely to succeed within camp and on campaign.
