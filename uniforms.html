Uniforms & Equipment

Field Instructions For Soldiers In Active Service

Introduction

Every soldier depended upon his clothing, weapon, and equipment for survival. Proper maintenance of supplies was necessary during marches, camp life, and battle.

The following guide explains the standard uniforms, essential field gear, and common shortages experienced during the Civil War.

General Rules For Equipment

  1. Keep rifles and weapons clean at all times.
  2. Carry only necessary equipment during long marches.
  3. Protect blankets, shoes, and canteens from damage.
  4. Inspect cartridge boxes and ammunition regularly.
  5. Maintain proper military appearance whenever possible.

Standard Uniforms

Union and Confederate military uniforms.

Union soldiers generally wore blue wool uniforms, while Confederate troops commonly wore gray or butternut clothing. Supply shortages caused uniforms to vary greatly as the war continued.

  • Blue Union uniforms
  • Gray and butternut Confederate clothing
  • Kepis and slouch hats
  • Rank insignia and brass buttons

Essential Equipment

Equipment Purpose
Rifled Musket Main infantry weapon used during battle.
Canteen Carried water during marches and combat.
Haversack Held food and personal supplies.
Blanket Roll Provided warmth and shelter in camp.
Cartridge Box Stored ammunition for the soldier's rifle.

Supply Problems & Shortages

Soldiers wearing worn and patched clothing.

Confederate armies often experienced severe shortages of uniforms, shoes, blankets, and weapons. Soldiers repaired damaged clothing and frequently used captured supplies.

  • Patched and repaired uniforms
  • Captured equipment from enemy forces
  • Mixed civilian and military clothing
  • Uneven quality of supplies

The Soldier's Load

Infantry soldiers often carried between thirty and forty pounds of equipment during campaigns.

  • Weapons and ammunition
  • Blankets and shelter materials
  • Food and cooking supplies
  • Personal belongings

During long marches, soldiers frequently discarded unnecessary items to reduce weight and exhaustion.

Final Notes

Uniforms and equipment affected a soldier's mobility, endurance, and battlefield readiness throughout the war.

Proper care of supplies often meant the difference between comfort and hardship during military service.
